We build privacy-first legal technology with a simple conviction: understanding what you are signing shouldn't require a law degree, or a credit card. After watching friends and freelancers get burned by clauses buried on page nine, we built DocuClerk to put a lawyer's first-pass instincts into everyone's hands, entirely free. Your contract is analyzed on your own device rather than on a server, and every feature ships to every user. That transparency isn't a business model; it's the point.

Under the hood

How it works

Our own engine, running entirely on your machine.

1

Extract and chunk

Your PDF, DOCX, or TXT is converted to text and split into overlapping windows so no clause slips between the cracks.

2

Clause detection

Our own engine scans each window for 41 clause categories, from non-competes to uncapped liability, entirely on your own CPU. Nothing is uploaded and nothing is queued behind other users.

3

Risk scoring

Detected clauses are grouped by severity and rolled into one clear safety score, with every flag linked back to the exact text that triggered it.

41 clauses, checked every single time

A human reader gets tired by page nine. DocuClerk doesn't. Our engine is built and maintained in-house, runs on your own machine, and checks the same 41 clause categories every time, the ones that most often cost people money, leverage, or their own work. A few of them:

Non-compete Exclusivity Uncapped liability IP assignment Auto-renewal Liquidated damages Indemnification Non-solicit Termination for convenience Governing law + 31 more
